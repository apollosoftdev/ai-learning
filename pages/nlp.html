Natural Language Processing

How AI understands and generates human language

What is Natural Language Processing?

Natural Language Processing (NLP)

A branch of artificial intelligence that enables computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language in a way that is both meaningful and useful. NLP bridges the gap between human communication and computer understanding.

NLP combines linguistics, computer science, and machine learning to process and analyze large amounts of natural language data. It's the technology behind voice assistants, translation services, and chatbots.

Core NLP Tasks

Tokenization

Breaking text into individual words, phrases, or sentences (tokens). The first step in most NLP pipelines.

"Hello world" -> ["Hello", "world"]

Part-of-Speech Tagging

Identifying grammatical parts of speech (noun, verb, adjective, etc.) for each word in a sentence.

"The cat sits" -> [DET, NOUN, VERB]

Named Entity Recognition

Identifying and classifying named entities (people, places, organizations, dates) in text.

"Apple is in Cupertino" -> [ORG, LOC]

Sentiment Analysis

Determining the emotional tone or opinion expressed in text (positive, negative, neutral).

"I love this!" -> Positive (0.95)

Machine Translation

Automatically translating text from one language to another while preserving meaning.

"Hello" (EN) -> "Hola" (ES)

Text Summarization

Condensing long documents into shorter summaries while retaining key information.

Natural Language Generation (NLG)

NLG vs NLU

NLU (Understanding): Computer reads and comprehends human text

NLG (Generation): Computer produces human-readable text from data

NLG is the process of producing meaningful phrases and sentences in natural language from internal data representation. It's the technology that enables AI to write articles, create reports, and have conversations.

NLP Applications

Search Engines

Understanding search queries, matching intent, and ranking relevant results. Powers Google, Bing, and other search systems.

Email & Spam Filtering

Classifying emails, detecting spam, smart compose suggestions, and priority inbox features.

Voice Assistants

Speech recognition, intent understanding, and voice-based interaction. Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant.

Social Media Analysis

Monitoring brand mentions, analyzing public opinion, detecting trends, and content moderation.

Challenges in NLP

Ambiguity

Words and sentences can have multiple meanings. "Bank" can mean financial institution or river bank. Context is crucial.

Language Diversity

7000+ languages worldwide, each with unique grammar, idioms, and cultural context. Most NLP research focuses on English.

Sarcasm & Irony

Understanding when someone means the opposite of what they say. "Oh great, another meeting" might be negative.

Bias

Models can learn and amplify biases present in training data, leading to unfair or discriminatory outputs.
