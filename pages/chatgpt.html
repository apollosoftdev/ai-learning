ChatGPT & Large Language Models

How GPT architecture powers conversational AI

What is ChatGPT?

ChatGPT

A conversational AI system built on OpenAI's GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) architecture. It generates human-like text responses by predicting the most likely next tokens based on the input context, trained on vast amounts of text data and refined through human feedback.

Released in November 2022, ChatGPT rapidly became the fastest-growing consumer application in history, reaching 100 million users in just two months. It demonstrated that large language models could engage in natural conversation, write code, explain complex topics, and assist with a wide range of tasks.

ChatGPT belongs to the family of Large Language Models (LLMs) — neural networks with billions of parameters trained on massive text datasets. Other notable LLMs include Claude (Anthropic), Gemini (Google), and LLaMA (Meta).

How ChatGPT Works: Step by Step

1

Tokenization

Your input text is split into tokens (subwords). "ChatGPT" might become ["Chat", "G", "PT"]. GPT-4 uses ~100K token vocabulary via Byte Pair Encoding (BPE).

2

Embedding

Each token is converted into a high-dimensional vector (12,288 dimensions in GPT-4). Positional encodings are added so the model knows word order.

3

Self-Attention

The transformer computes attention scores between all tokens, learning which words relate to each other. "The cat sat on the mat" — "sat" attends strongly to "cat".

4

Feed-Forward

After attention, each position passes through a feed-forward network that applies non-linear transformations, enabling the model to capture complex patterns.

5

Layer Stacking

Steps 3-4 repeat through 96+ transformer layers. Early layers capture syntax, middle layers semantics, and deep layers complex reasoning.

6

Token Generation

The final layer outputs a probability over all tokens. Sampling (with temperature control) picks the next token. This repeats autoregressively until done.

Training Process

ChatGPT's training happens in three key stages, each building on the previous one:

Pre-training

Next-token prediction on trillions of words from the internet

SFT

Supervised Fine-Tuning on human-written Q&A demonstrations

RLHF

Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback for alignment

Pre-training

The model reads trillions of tokens from books, websites, and code. It learns language patterns by predicting the next word in sequences. This is unsupervised and requires massive compute (thousands of GPUs for months).

Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT)

Human trainers write ideal responses to prompts. The model is fine-tuned on these demonstrations, learning the format, tone, and helpfulness expected of an AI assistant.

RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback)

Human raters rank multiple model outputs from best to worst. A reward model learns these preferences, then PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization) trains the model to maximize the reward, aligning it with human values.

What ChatGPT Can Do

Conversation

Natural multi-turn dialogue, Q&A, debate, role-playing, and brainstorming sessions.

Code Generation

Write, debug, refactor, and explain code across dozens of programming languages.

Writing

Essays, emails, reports, creative fiction, marketing copy, and technical documentation.

Analysis

Data interpretation, document summarization, sentiment analysis, and research synthesis.

Translation

Translate between 100+ languages with nuanced understanding of context and idioms.

Education

Tutoring, concept explanation, quiz generation, and personalized learning paths.

Key Limitations

Hallucinations

LLMs can generate plausible-sounding but factually incorrect information with confidence. They predict likely text patterns, not verified facts.

Knowledge Cutoff

Models have a training data cutoff date and don't know about events after that date unless augmented with retrieval tools or web browsing.

Reasoning Gaps

While improving rapidly, LLMs can struggle with complex multi-step math, logic puzzles, and tasks requiring true causal understanding.
